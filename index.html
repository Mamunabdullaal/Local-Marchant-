Rejection Reasons

New Reason Options for Rejecting Merchants

When "Received all/part of the purchased item" is selected on the annotation ticket, customer service needs to choose a reason to reject the merchant. This rejection reason will be displayed on the merchant page along with the required supplementary materials the merchant must provide.

[Updated on September 6, 2025] Added a new option: [The merchant's evidence is valid]. [Reasons for rejection] will now prioritize options relevant to cases where the merchant's warehouse has not issued a refund. Selecting [Other reasons] will display all available options.

Annotation Options

Customer Evidence Anomalies

Risk control requires data support to identify abnormal customer behavior. Therefore, customer service needs to label whether the merchant's feedback on such behavior is valid. The annotation options are "Yes, the customer's behavior is abnormal", "No, the customer's behavior is not abnormal", "Unable to determine"

Abnormal behaviors include:
Abnormal Behavior Definition
Returned trash only The return package contains only low-value items such as sand, stones, or tissue bags; or contains only used daily consumables such as hair gel or vinegar.
Returned old items for new purchase Returned items show significant differences in condition, with clear signs of long-term use.
Returned inferior items as originals Returned items differ from the original shipment in brand or model, with a significant value gap.
Returned an empty package The package is completely empty; any contents make this invalid.
Tampered with shipping information The shipping label shows clear signs of tampering, or the logistics provider confirms the customer altered delivery information.
Frequent or bulk returns A customer makes frequent purchases and returns in a short period; merchants may also report cases of malicious competition or reselling.
Other Other scenarios the merchant considers abnormal.
Note: You can assess the merchant's description to determine if the behavior involves returning trash, returning old for new, returning inferior items, sending back empty packages, tampering with shipping information, or excessive and frequent returns.

Items Not Returned

The customer has mailed the package, and tracking shows it has been delivered. The merchant's warehouse claims not to have received the package.

If the following points are proven, please label it as "All items not received":
  • Incorrect return address leading to the warehouse not receiving the package (proof of package received at a non-warehouse address)
[Updated on July 17, 2024] Background: Some online merchants prove that the delivery address is inconsistent, but in fact, it is found that the warehouse address has been modified by the merchant, causing the user's package to be sent to the previous warehouse address.
What's updated in the workbench: Historical warehouse address & latest warehouse address will be shown in the after-sales order. You need to verify whether the delivery address of the returned package matches the historical and latest warehouse addresses shown in the Workbench.
[Updated Sep 19, 2024] If the Temu return label is used but the tracking shows the delivery address was changed (e.g., "the delivery address has been updated"), it suggests the wrong return address was provided due to the user's actions. Support the merchant and mark the single package in question as "All items not received (or returned items are not the purchased items)".
[Updated on Aug. 8, 2025] For some carriers, such as J&T Express, the delivery location (province/state/city) may not be identifiable in the signed logistics racking on the Workbench. Customer service may refer to 17track or the carrier's official website for the delivery address.
[Updated on Sep. 1, 2025] If the evidence provided by the merchant (such as an email or statement from the carrier) confirms that the user tampered with the return information, and the tracking number in the evidence matches the return tracking number, with the carrier's official logo included, then the package is considered not returned to the warehouse. Mark the single package in question as "All items not received (or returned items are not the purchased items)".
The following content CANNOT be used as valid evidence:
  • Information from third-party platforms (e.g., proof from Amazon that their return warehouse didn't receive the package)
  • Screenshots showing the warehouse query page can't find the package or the return tracking number information
  • The tracking number in the proof of delivery (POD) provided by the merchant does not match the return tracking number for this order
Standard Handling Procedures:
I. Annotation ticked has only one linked return package (single package)
  1. If the merchant claims they haven't received the package, first check the delivery tracking for any rejection records:
    • If a rejection is recorded, verify whether the rejection address exactly matches the merchant's historical or latest warehouse addresses.
      • If it matches, consider the package intentionally rejected by the merchant (thus already returned to the warehouse), and mark [Received all/part of the purchased item]
      • If it does not match, proceed to the next step
    • If there's no rejection record, or the rejection record lacks address information, proceed to the next step
  2. Please check whether the country/state or province of the return delivery address matches the country/state or province of the merchant's historical warehouse address/latest warehouse address. There is no need to check at the city level.
    • If they don't match, support the merchant, consider the package as not returned to the warehouse, and mark [All items not received]
    • If they match, proceed to the next step
  3. If the POD provided by the seller or the tracking information on the carrier's official website includes a detailed delivery address, check the distance between the package's delivery address and the seller's address using Google Maps. If the distance is less than 5 miles, support the user, and consider the package as returned to the warehouse. Mark the case as [Received all/part of the purchased item]
  4. If the POD provided by the seller or the tracking information on the carrier's official website shows no detailed address and only includes regional information:
    1. Check if the merchant is tagged "Merchants with high rejection rate". If yes, consider the package as returned to the warehouse, and mark the case as [Received all/part of the purchased item]. If no, proceed to step 2)
    2. The user is C3-C5 not flagged for risk control, and there are records of sender-side address changes in the tracking, support the user, consider the package as returned to the warehouse, and mark the case as [Received all/part of the purchased item]
Note: In some cases, carriers may correct or adjust the city name, which can result in the recorded delivery city being different from the warehouse city, even though the package has in fact been delivered to the correct location. When the delivery address provided is approximate or not fully detailed, customer service agents only need to verify whether the country and state/province match. There is no need to compare the city information.
Note: After labeling it as [received all/part of the purchased goods], please follow Step 2 to determine whether the goods are damaged and can be sold again. (In this scenario, there are generally no supporting documents for the conditions of the returned goods, so it can be labeled as [number of items not affecting resale])
II. Annotation ticked has multiple linked return packages

Use the list of return tracking numbers to determine if multiple packages are involved.

In cases with multiple packages, check whether each package has been received by the warehouse and mark the case based on how many received items affect resale:

Examples:
  • If 5 packages were supposed to be returned and none were received, mark "All items not received (or returned items are not the purchased items)"
  • If 5 packages were supposed to be returned and 4 were received while 1 was not, mark "Received all/part of the purchased item"
    • For the 4 received packages, indicate how many items affect resale and how many do not
    • There's no need to mark the unreturned package for resale impact
Large items split into multiple packages

When a large item is returned in multiple packages and the merchant has only received some of them, the handling rules are as follows:

  • The merchant must provide photos or videos of the received packages. You then use this evidence to determine whether the contents will affect the resale of the item (e.g., if a package contains only part of the expected components)
  • If the merchant fails to provide evidence or the evidence is invalid, making it impossible to determine whether the received package contains the complete item or only partial components, the item should be marked as not affecting resale

Empty Package

The merchant's warehouse has received the return package. Photo/video evidence shows that the package did not contain the actual product:

  • The package was completely empty
  • The package only contained an empty product box
  • The package only contained sand, tissues, or other low-value fillers
[Sep. 3, 2025 Update] Standard Handling Procedure:
If the following two points can be proven, mark as [All items not received (or returned items are not the purchased items)]:
  1. The package tracking number / order ID on the return label photo matches the return details in the Workbench, proving that the package was indeed sent by the customer.
    • For returns to self-operated warehouses, this requirement can be relaxed. It is not mandatory for the warehouse inspection video/photo to display the logistics tracking number on the package
  2. Photo/video evidence of the return package proves that the actual product was not inside the package.
    • For example: the package was completely empty; the package only contained an empty product box; the package only contained sand, tissues, or other low-value fillers
Special Case

When the customer states in their after-sales request that the received package contained only an empty product box, and therefore only returned the empty product box:

  1. First determine whether the customer has provided video/photo evidence of receiving an empty product box. If provided, proceed to the next step. If not provided, please handle according to the standard procedure
  2. In addition to the return label photo and return package photo/video, if the merchant can provide shipping proof showing that the product was shipped normally (shipping proof requirements below), then mark as [All items not received (or returned items are not the purchased items)]. Otherwise, mark as [Received all/part of the purchased item]
Merchant's shipping proof only needs to meet one of the following conditions:
  • Shipping video/shipping photo showing the tracking number, and that the shipped product was not an empty product box
  • Shipping list, which must include product information, along with either the shipping tracking number or the Order ID
  • Shipping proof/logistics official website/shipping label showing the tracking number and the package weight ≥ 80% of the estimated package weight
[updated on July 9] Merchant supporting documents & package weight as shown on carrier website
Background: The package weight shown on some carriers' (e.g., UPS) websites is exactly the same as the estimated weight shown on the return label provided by merchants. These packages were not actually weighted when they were shipped. Hence below rules are introduced.
If the merchant uses screenshots of the weight of the return package on the official carrier website and shows that the weight is exactly the same as the weight predicted by the merchant on the workbench, the proof will be deemed invalid and the label should be "Received all/part of the purchased goods"
Example: (1700g = 3.8LBS)
Note: After labeling it as [received all/part of the purchased goods], please follow Step 2 to determine whether the goods are damaged and can be sold again.

Item Mismatch

The merchant's warehouse has received the return package. The returned item doesn't match the ordered item's type/model.

If the following points are proven, please label it as "All items not received (or returned items are not the purchased items)":
  • The signed package is indeed sent by the user (the package tracking number / order ID on the return label photo matches the return details in the Workbench; return package photos)
    • Note: If it is returned to a self-operated warehouse, this standard can be relaxed appropriately, and it is not mandatory for the quality inspection video/photo of the self-operated warehouse to show the package's logistics label
  • The item or item model doesn't match the order (return item photos)
    • If the returned item's appearance doesn't match the product details, it can be judged that the returned item doesn't match the ordered item
    • For digital devices like 3C products and iPhones, if the merchant can prove that the SN or IMEI code of the returned item does not match the one shipped, the case may be marked as "All items not received (or returned items are not the purchased items)"
Required evidence (the merchant needs to provide both the shipment and return evidence):
  1. Shipment evidence: Photos or videos of shipment, or a seller note on the order page showing the item's SN or IMEI code. If there are multiple seller notes, the one recorded closest to but before the shipment time will be considered valid
  2. Return evidence: Photos or videos of the returned item clearly showing its SN or IMEI code, proving that it differs from the one on the shipped item
Note: If unproven, please label it as "Received all/part of the purchased items"
Note: After labeling it as [received all/part of the purchased goods], please follow Step 2 to determine whether the goods are damaged and can be sold again.
If the customer requests for wrong item after-sales

The annotation item may not match the item evidenced by the warehouse. In this scenario, if the reason for non-refund from the merchant's warehouse is [mismatch of returned items], please judge whether all the purchased items have been received by combining the customer's after-sale request/appeal record and the warehouse's evidence:

Step1.1 Check the customer's request record to determine whether the returned items are consistent with the wrong items described in the customer's after-sale request/appeal record:
  • If it is not consistent, then mark [All items have not been received (or none of the returned items are the purchased items)]
Step1.2 If the item returned by the customer is the same as the item described in the customer's after-sales request/appeal record, check whether the warehouse has any proof that no wrong items have been shipped (any one of the following is acceptable):
  • Merchant's shipping video: Must clearly show the items inside the package and the tracking number to confirm the package's identity
  • Shipping photo and shipping label photo: The shipping photo must clearly show the items inside the package, and the tracking number on the label must match the tracking number in the order
  • Shipping list: For example, a screenshot of a third-party platform's shipping details page, including the product name(s) and tracking details

If the warehouse can provide any of the above evidence that the correct item was shipped, label the case as [All items not received (or returned items are not the purchased items)]

Step1.3 If the warehouse cannot provide any of the above evidence, or the provided evidence is invalid, label the case as [Received all/part of the purchased item]
Note: After labeling it as [received all/part of the purchased goods], please follow Step 2 to determine whether the goods are damaged and can be sold again.

Correct Item Sent

Exceptional circumstances: If the merchant's reason for rejection is [correct item was sent], it is possible that the merchant did not make a mistake, and the user placed the wrong order and mistakenly thought the wrong item was sent.

Please make a comprehensive judgment based on the notes and the PO order details below.

The merchant warehouse has received the user's return package. The returned item does not match the item type/model of the order.

Check if the warehouse has evidence that the correct items were sent (any one of the following is acceptable):
  • Merchant's shipping video: Must clearly show the items inside the package and the tracking number to confirm the package's identity
  • Shipping photo and shipping label photo: The shipping photo must clearly show the items inside the package, and the tracking number on the label must match the tracking number in the order
  • Shipping list: For example, a screenshot of a third-party platform's shipping details page, including the product name(s) and tracking details

If the warehouse can provide any of the above evidence that the correct item was shipped, label the case as [All items not received (or returned items are not the purchased items)]

If the warehouse has evidence that the correct items were sent (such as a video/screenshot of the packaging), and the evidence can prove that the items sent by the merchant are the same as those purchased by the user:
  • The package sent is indeed the package of the user's complaint order (return package label; return package photo)
    • Note: If it is returned to a self-operated warehouse, this standard can be relaxed appropriately, and it is not mandatory for the quality inspection video/photo of the self-operated warehouse to show the package's logistics label
Step 1.2 If the item returned by the customer is the same as the item described in the customer's after-sales request/appeal record, check whether the warehouse has any evidence that the correct item was sent.
  • Merchant's shipping video: Must clearly show the items inside the package and the tracking number to confirm the package's identity
  • Shipping photo and shipping label photo: The shipping photo must clearly show the items inside the package, and the tracking number on the label must match the tracking number in the order
  • Shipping list: For example, a screenshot of a third-party platform's shipping details page, including the product name(s) and tracking details

If the warehouse cannot provide any of the above evidence, or the provided evidence is invalid, label the case as [Received all/part of the purchased item]

Note: After marking [Received all/part of the purchased items], please further determine whether the items are damaged and affect resale according to Step 2.

Item Damaged

The merchant's warehouse has received the return package. The item is damaged, or missing major accessories, affecting secondary sales.

Customer Service Review Process for User Evidence:

Please review the images or videos in the user's after-sales request or chat history to determine whether the item was damaged or had quality issues upon delivery.

Note: When an agent submits a return on behalf of the user, images may not be uploaded, but the user may have already provided them in the chat history. Please check the chat records.

  1. If the user provides no evidence, or evidence unrelated to the after-sales item, then rely on the merchant's evidence to determine whether the product affects resale
  2. If the user provides evidence showing the item was damaged or missing parts upon delivery, support the user (if not C1 and cleared by risk control) regardless of whether their evidence exactly matches the merchant's, and mark as [Number of items not affecting resale]. Otherwise, determine whether the item affects resale based on the merchant's evidence
  3. If the user's photos/videos show no obvious damage or missing parts, but the merchant's evidence does:
    1. If the user provides a packing video recorded before sending the return, proving the item was intact at the time of return, support the user and mark as [Number of items not affecting resale]
    2. If the user provides non-packing video evidence but the merchant provides a complete unboxing video, rely on the merchant's evidence to decide whether the product affects resale
    3. If both sides provide other types of videos or only photos:
      • For single items priced under 150 USD, if the user passes risk control and is not categorized as C1, support the user and mark as [Number of items not affecting resale]. Otherwise, rely on the merchant's evidence to judge resale impact
      • For single items priced at or above 150 USD, if the user passes risk control and is categorized as C3–C5, support the user and mark as [Number of items not affecting resale]. Otherwise, rely on the merchant's evidence to determine resale impact
If the following points are proven, please label it as "Number of items affecting resale":
  • The signed package is indeed sent by the user (the package tracking number / order ID on the return label photo matches the return details in the Workbench; return package photos)
    • Note: If it is returned to a Temu-managed warehouse, this standard can be relaxed appropriately, and it is not mandatory for the quality inspection video/photo of the Temu-managed warehouse to show the package's logistics label
  • The item is indeed damaged (detailed photos/videos of the returned item)
    • e.g., Clear usage marks on the item, such as a rusted pot
    • The damaged item is part of the order (overall photos of the returned item)
    • Note: The tracking number in the image below has been obscured for privacy protection. For actual evidence submission, the full shipping label must be visible
    • For tires, the following conditions may affect their resale:
      • Tread edges are dulled or the vent spews are worn. (If only the vent spews are missing the tread itself remains intact, resale is not affected)
      • Stubborn dirt, asphalt, oil, or other hard-to-remove stains
      • Obvious signs of use on the rim or tread, including scratches, cuts, deformities due to pressure, or installation marks
Note: Normal unboxing as a common step in online shopping does not count as damage. These should be labeled as "Number of items not affecting resale". Examples:
  • Outer packaging (such as cartons or plastic bags) has tears or scratches
  • Soles of shoes show slight dust
  • Clothes have creases from packaging or shipping
  • Accessories like screws or wheels show minor installation or trial-use marks
  • Wipeable stains, such as light dust, surface dirt, fibers, or hair
Note: If proof doesn't show the returned item is damaged, please label it as "Number of items not affecting resale"

High Rejection Rate Merchants

Annotation Rules for Merchants with High Rejection Rates

Background: For "Local merchants / semi-managed" orders, in cases of the rejection in second review that result in external complaints, it was found that some merchants deliberately acted maliciously, resulting in unreasonable refusal of after-sales service; therefore, the [high rejection rate merchant] label was launched to provide a warning.
Commonalities of such merchants: They failed to provide a clear and visible return label, making it impossible to prove that the package was indeed returned by the user, and the same evidence was used in multiple cases.
Reasons involved to reject after-sales services Features of evidence provided
  • Package returned by the user is empty
  • Returned item does not match
  • Returned item is damaged
  • Only one picture of an empty package is provided, but it cannot be proven that it was sent back by the user in the current case
  • Only one return package unboxing video is provided, but the return label information is not clearly visible
  • The merchant provided 1 return label and 1 photo of the package after it was opened, but the two cannot be linked
  • The merchant provided a return label that was almost brand new and a video of a return package being opened, but the return label in that video was not legible, and the two could not be linked, nor could it be proven that the return was sent by the current user
  • One picture of a damaged / obviously used product is provided, but it cannot be proven that it was sent back by the current user in this case
Where is the label: It is right beside the name of the merchant on the annotation workbench, under annotation tasks under the category "Local Merchats - Warehouse quality inspection of consumer returns - Customer Service Second Review", seen in the following screenshots.
Processing standards for annotation tickets:
① When the label "high rejection rate merchant" is applied

In the rejection scenarios of "returning an empty package", "returning a mismatched item", and "having already sent the correct item", in addition to meeting the evidence requirements listed in the "5.4.2 [Local Merchants] Second Review of Merchant Appeal for Allegedly Missing Items in Returned Goods" under "5. Standards for Evidence", all of the following conditions must also be met in order to annotate the case as "None of the items has been received (or the returned items are not the purchased items)"; otherwise, it must be annotated as "All/some of the purchased items have been received."

Required conditions:
  • A clearly visible return label is provided, which matches the "return tracking number" displayed on the workbench
  • It can be proven that the returned items do not match or that the return package is empty, and that this was verified by unpacking the package returned by the user. (The unpacking video must be complete and continuous, with the entire unpacking process visible throughout)
② When the label "high return rejection rate merchant" is applied

In the scenario of "Returned item damaged" / other reasons for rejection, in addition to meeting the evidence requirements listed in the "5.4.2 [Local Merchants] Second Review of Merchant Appeal for Allegedly Missing Items in Returned Goods" under "5. Standards for Evidence", all of the following conditions must also be met in order to annotate it as "All / some of the purchased items have been received", and fill in the "Number of items that affect resale" and "Number of items that do not affect resale" based on facts. Otherwise, "All / some of the purchased items have been received" and "Number of items that affect resale" should be annotated as 0.

Required conditions:
  • A clearly visible return label is provided, which matches the "return tracking number" displayed on the workbench
  • It can be proven that the returned items damaged / the returned items affect resale, and that this was verified by unpacking the package returned by the user. (The unpacking video must be complete and continuous, with the entire unpacking process visible throughout)
